Building Our Lead-to-Cash Process

1. Scope and Prioritize

The scope of this process begins with the first marketing touchpoint with a potential client and ends with the successful collection of payment for services rendered. We prioritize this flow because it is our primary value chain and directly impacts customer experience and revenue generation. It represents our core promise to the market.

2. Identify and Engage Stakeholders

This process involves collaboration across multiple functions. Key stakeholders include:

  • Clients: The recipients of our services and the focus of our value delivery.
  • Sales & Marketing Team: Responsible for lead generation, nurturing, and closing deals.
  • Training & Consulting Team: The subject matter experts who define and deliver the services.
  • Finance & Admin Team: Responsible for contracts, invoicing, collections, and financial reporting.
  • Management: Provides oversight, strategy, and resources.

3. Identify Process Components

The process is built from components within our SOP Framework, which are documented through workshops with subject matter experts from each functional team. The primary components are:

  • Market and Sell Products and Services (3.0): Creating awareness and generating sales orders.
  • Deliver Services (5.0): Fulfilling the sales order by delivering high-quality training.
  • Perform Revenue Accounting (9.2): Managing the financial settlement of the service.

4. Apply a Life Cycle Perspective

We view this process through a complete life cycle lens to ensure a seamless customer experience. This means managing the hand-offs between teams effectively—from sales to delivery, and from delivery to finance. The goal is to create a single, unified journey for the client, where each stage builds upon the last, reinforcing the value and professionalism of Valor Egypt.
